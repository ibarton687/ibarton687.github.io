SIM3 Project · Robotics

Mechanical Arm
& Gripper

Robotics 6-DoF SolidWorks FEA Electromechanical

Overview

In SIM2 we built an autonomous line-following vehicle. SIM3 was the next step — mount a robotic arm on top of it. The task: the vehicle drives a course, spots a diabolo on the field, picks it up, and places it at a target location. All without human input.

The arm is 6-DoF, driven by stepper motors, with a 3-bearing support structure and a custom aluminium frame. I was involved from the first concept sketch through to the final physical build.

My Role

I started on the arm concept and early prototype — CAD, drawings, FEA. As the project matured I helped design components for the real arm and was part of the team that built and wired the whole thing. Fabrication, assembly, electronics — the full process.

Technical Work

  • Designed the initial arm concept in SolidWorks with full engineering drawings
  • Performed FEA-based stress and displacement analysis to validate structural integrity under load
  • Contributed to the design of components for the final real arm
  • Integrated the stepper motor with a 3-bearing support system for low-friction, precise rotation
  • Helped with aluminium fabrication — cutting, drilling, and assembling structural components
  • Helped wire and assemble the full electromechanical system through to final build
  • Supported gripper setup and end-effector integration

Outcome

Contributed to delivering a fully functional robotic arm — from early concept and structural analysis through to the final physical build. Gained hands-on experience in electromechanical integration, low-friction mechanism design, and the full engineering process from CAD to real hardware.
