Fontys University · Sep 2024 – Present

Torpedo Kid
EV Chassis

Electric Vehicle Chassis Design FEA Battery Packaging SolidWorks

Overview

The Torpedo Kid is a road-legal electric vehicle being designed and built by a student team at Fontys University. The project operates with a €20,000 budget and industry partnerships with EV Europe and 247TailorSteel. I joined in September 2024 and own the middle chassis section and battery placement.

My Role

Middle chassis design, battery packaging, structural analysis, and safety integration. The core challenge: fitting large battery packs and drivetrain components into a constrained mid-frame while maintaining structural rigidity, crash safety, and road legality.

Technical Work

  • Developed the mid-frame in CAD — modelling structural members, mounting points, and component clearances
  • Designed battery placement within strict spatial and weight distribution constraints
  • Performed FEA-based stress, torsional, and structural analysis in SolidWorks to validate safety
  • Contributed to battery protection concepts and crash safety integration
  • Collaborated with EV Europe and 247TailorSteel on material and fabrication decisions

Challenge

Packaging — fitting large batteries and drivetrain components within the mid-frame while keeping it safe, functional, and road-legal. Every millimetre matters when balancing structural integrity against component access and weight distribution.
