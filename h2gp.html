Personal Project · 2024

H2GP Hydrogen
RC Car

Hydrogen Systems Thermal Design CAD Competition Forbes Featured

Overview

A hydrogen-powered RC race car designed and built for the H2GP competition — a national and international STEM racing challenge. Our 5-person team from Prague British International School had just 6 months to design, build, and train, while competing teams had years of experience.

My Role

Mechanical build, fuel cell cooling, hydrogen routing, and testing. My primary focus was the thermal management of the fuel cell — a critical system that directly determines performance and longevity of the hydrogen propulsion unit under race conditions.

Technical Work

  • Designed heat sinks for the fuel cell system to manage thermal load during operation
  • Adjusted steering geometry and modified chassis dimensions to fit two hydrogen fuel sticks
  • Optimised hydrogen line routing to the fuel cell, balancing weight, packaging, and safety
  • Made engineering trade-offs between weight, power output, and system efficiency
  • Assembled the complete car and led pre-race testing and troubleshooting

Press

The team was featured in Forbes Czech Republic under the headline "Clean ride to Vegas. Prague students compete with hydrogen car model" — including a personal comment from each team member.
